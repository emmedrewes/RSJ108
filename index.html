Emerson Drewes' Tips on How to Further your Journalism Career

A WikiHow for College Students

How to gain more experience in the field of Journalism as a student.

Assembly time: All the time in the world!
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

Are you a student and struggling to gain experience in the Journalism field? Well you came to the right place! In this Wikihow I will give you the "How To" on how to start in the field of journalism. By the end of this list you are sure to feel more ready to go out and be a journalist.

  • Things You'll Need

  • Laptop
  • Internet Connection
  • Pen
  • Paper

  1. Go to events designed for journalists.

    When you are a student, you can use all of the help you can get. Good thing your university is here to help you! Chances are your school holds lectures from experienced journalists to provide you with guidance. You are sure to gain more knowledge when you do this!

  2. Stay up-to-date on what popular journalists are doing today.

    Who would have guessed that something as big as a newspaper can now fit into the palms of our hands. That is why this step is so important! Journalism as a field is constantly evolving, so it is the journalist job to evolve with the times and with its readers. Head over to the Pulitzer website to see the current winners and why they are so acclaimed in today's world.

    As mentioned in one of our RSJ 108 lectures, The New York Times has continued to revolutionze media as we know it

  3. Write!

    This one seems self explanatory, but as a journalist you want to make sure your fingers are always moving. Grab a pen and paper and write a letter to a loved one. Jot down some notes of interesting things you see in daily life. Make a medium account and keep writing stories.

    • Grab a pen and paper and write a letter to a loved one.
    • Jot down some notes of interesting things you see in everyday life.
    • Create an account on Medium.com so you can continue to write stories when you are not in school.
    Here is a quick video on how to write a lead just in case you forget!

  4. Participate in student run media.

    Almost every university has opportunities for students to get involved with the media production. The University of Nevada, Reno has some amazing student run media that you can volunteer at or work for. These options include: The Nevada Sagebrush, Insight Magazine, Brushfire, and Wolfpack Radio.

    • The Nevada Sagebrush
    • Insight Magazine
    • Brushfire
    • Wolfpack Radio

  5. Learn skills that will make you stand out.

    Pick up a camera and take powerful photos at protests. Learn a new language. Learn how to code! All of these things will make you look more desirable to a potential employer and you are sure to have fun while doing it!
